Overview

BirSé is a Bulgarian fine-jewelry house - gold, silver, and natural stones - online since 2013, with stores in Sofia.

The brief was pointed: small UI changes, drastic UX improvements. Not a rebuild - a strategic visual elevation of a live store that already ranks and sells.

The Challenge

80% of traffic is mobile - mostly women aged 45-64, often arriving from a Google ad. The live store couldn't be rebuilt, its SEO had to survive, and Bulgaria's 2026 euro switch made dual-currency pricing a legal requirement. Every decision was judged on a 375px screen first.

The Approach: Warm Luxury

Clean, but not cold. Generous whitespace, DM Serif Display headlines, a muted gold accent, and red used with restraint - because luxury is restraint. Designed for a 50-year-old on an iPhone, not a design-award jury.

The Solution

Six redesigned flows, every one evaluated on mobile first.

  • Mobile navigation, done right A full-screen drill-down drawer - max two taps to any subcategory. Visible navigation beats a buried hamburger for this audience.
  • Filters as a bottom sheet The single biggest mobile win: the product grid shows by default, filters slide up on demand with live result counts so no one fears a zero-result dead end.
  • A product page built to convert Ruthless hierarchy, trust chips above the fold, and TBI installments placed at the exact moment of price hesitation, with a sticky add-to-cart bar.
  • Checkout that respects Bulgaria A true multi-step flow: delivery method first (Еконт / Спиди / BoxNow offices), cash-on-delivery as default, and the promo field hidden so shoppers don't leave to hunt for a code.
  • Legally-safe price cards Euro-first dual currency at equal weight, EU-Omnibus compliant - the discounted price in red, no fake-urgency badges or phantom strikethroughs.
  • A signature stone system Natural-stone gems rendered as radial-gradient orbs, colour-dotted for instant scanning - the brand's USP made visual.

Mobile carries the store

Four in five visitors shop on a phone, so the phone got the best thinking: 16px type to prevent iOS zoom, 44px+ touch targets, familiar patterns over trendy ones, and filters and checkout reshaped to fit one thumb.

Checkout, rebuilt around how Bulgaria buys

Delivery method comes first - 90% of parcels go to an Еконт or Спиди office, so asking for a full address up front is backwards. Cash-on-delivery is the default, express pay sits at the top, and each step collapses to a summary as you go. Fewer fields, fewer reasons to leave.

The Look

DM Serif Display for headlines and italic product names; DM Sans everywhere else at a 16px floor. A warm off-white ground, a muted gold accent B8986A, a single confident red C4362A, and dark near-black CTAs that read as Cartier, not bargain bin.

What we're optimising for

The redesign is in development with PFG Bulgaria; the current site is still live. The targets are set, not yet measured: guest checkout to cut abandonment (Baymard puts the lift at 25-35%), proper event instrumentation so the wins are finally trackable, and a storefront shaped around the 80% who shop on a phone.
